Welcome to my website! I am a current PhD student in the Department of Agricultural and Resource Economics at the [University of Connecticut](https://are.uconn.edu). 

I am an applied microeconomist studying how economic shocks shape production, market outcomes, and price dynamics across developed and developing economies. My work focuses on how these shocks propagate from production decisions to market prices and welfare. I also study how market structure and market power mediate this transmission in agricultural and food markets.
My approach combines structural and reduced-form methods with tools from causal inference, spatial econometrics, and machine learning, using administrative, survey, and high-resolution geospatial data.

Outside of research, I enjoy running, watching documentaries, and reading crime fiction.
